# Guard ↔ Vendor-Native Validator Coverage Matrix

**Owner**: observability
**Date**: 2026-05-14
**Purpose**: Quantify the leverage of Option (D) ("shrink the surface area first") by inventorying every Python-using guard observability owns or co-owns against the relevant vendor-native validator. Resolves Round 1 Disagreement 1 ("how much does (D) actually shrink?").
**Scope**: 6 of the 8 Python-invoking guards under `scripts/guards/simple/`. The two doc-citation guards (Guard A `validate-doc-citations-no-line-numbers.sh`, Guard C `validate-doc-citations-symbol-resolves.sh`) are out of scope here — they have no vendor analogue (lazy-reason policy is repo-internal) and code-reviewer/dry-reviewer own the framing for those.

## Reading the matrix

- **What vendor covers**: the failure modes the vendor tool catches if invoked against the same input.
- **What vendor does NOT cover**: the bespoke policy layer that would remain in any thin-wrapper post-(D) rewrite.
- **LoC deletable**: lines that could be removed if we kept *only* the post-vendor residual. Estimates are best-effort against the current guard source; counts include comments/blank-lines proportionally.
- **LoC residual**: total LoC − deletable. The size of the Python surface (C) would have to formalize around even after maximal (D) shrinkage.
- **Can delete guard?**: yes / no / partial. A "yes" means the vendor covers everything load-bearing; the guard itself goes away. "Partial" means the vendor covers a large fraction and the residual is small enough to fold into a thin shell wrapper.

## Aggregate

Across the 5 guards observability owns directly (rows 1-5): **3,115 total LoC, ~25-50 deletable under maximal-(D) thin-wrapper, ~3,065-3,090 LoC residual = <2% surface reduction.**

Adding row 6 (observability-adjacent, ops co-owned): **3,719 total LoC, ~385-450 deletable, ~3,270-3,335 residual = ~10-12% surface reduction**, concentrated entirely in one guard (`grafana-datasources.sh`).

## Implications for the four end states

**(A) Standardize on Python**: still rewrites ~17 simple bash guards not in this matrix; not addressed by (D) leverage. Out of scope here.

**(B) Standardize on bash**: technically viable only for row 3 (`validate-infrastructure-metrics.sh`) at +LoC cost. Rows 1, 2, 4, 5 hit bash limits (balanced-paren walks, component-split denylists, four-way cross-references with state). Not addressed by this matrix; tracked separately.

**(C) Polyglot, formalized**: the surface that needs formalizing (lockfile / lint / audit / interpreter pin / import discipline) is ~3,065 LoC of Python regardless of (D). (D) does not defer the (C) toolchain question because <2% shrink doesn't materially reduce the formalization scope.

**(D) Shrink surface area first**:
- **Row 6 is the lone real (D) target** — Grafana provisioning dry-run replaces ~60% of `grafana-datasources.sh`. Recommend bundling this into the consensus branch (lands ~360-400 LoC deletion, ~30 LoC of bash wrapper added, net negative LoC).
- **Row 1 admits a complementary (D) addition** — `promtool check rules` runs *alongside* `validate-alert-rules.sh`, catching PromQL syntax errors the guard ignores. Adds coverage; deletes nothing. Recommend bundling.
- **Rows 2, 3, 4, 5 have no meaningful (D) lever.** Vendor tools cover orthogonal failure modes from what those guards actually check.

- "Deletable" estimates: best-effort attribution of LoC to checks; verified against guard source for each row (function-level scan of `validate-alert-rules.sh` confirms only ~10 LoC sit in YAML-parse-error path).
- "What vendor covers" claims for `promtool check rules` and Grafana provisioning dry-run: from observability domain knowledge of the tools' documented features; not re-verified against current upstream releases for this matrix. If any peer has concrete counter-evidence (e.g., a `promtool` feature flag that lints `for:` durations), please contest in Round 2 with the upstream documentation or feature flag.

Wholesale replacement of `validate-alert-rules.sh` by `promtool check rules` would silently regress this coverage.
